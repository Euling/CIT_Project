Meditation in itself is a personal journey that reflects on clearing your mind and focusing on each breath.

Meditation is such a personal, singular thing to do that it might be strange to think of it as a group activity. Although group meditation provides an opportunity to counteract some of negative effects of our paradoxically connected and lonesome experiences. When practicing meditation within a group setting, you’re able to feed off the energy of the group. As a way of sharing meaningful space with others, it touches us in ways that most other activities cannot. Some of these benefits include:

  • Group meditation can help with habit formation.
  • Meditating with others creates a sense of connectedness.
  • Group settings provide opportunity for sharing our experience and feedback.
  • Broadens our understanding of how we can share space.
  • Group meditation is an amazing opportunity to meet new people.

To help you start with a group meditation we found the best websites for you:

Insight Timer

The app’s Circles feature is a great way to connect with others — you can chat and meditate live with groups of friends, colleagues, or strangers. You can also find curated playlists with themes like releasing guilt, practicing compassion, and anxiety relief.

Headspace

Headspace is well-known for its expansive library of guided meditations and natural soundscapes, but its “Move Mode” makes it a standout among the rest. Get your blood flowing with workouts and mental fitness exercises inspired by professional athletes. Add your friends or work colleagues to the buddies feature and have the option of letting them see your progress.

Mindful Leader

Mindful Leader is online meditation sessions that are 30 minutes long, split into two halves. Classes are held using the zoom and are held every hour on each day. The first half is silent time for meditation and the second half is for reflection & sharing. They offer training, curate and convene events, learning communities and educational resources to inspire, develop, and enable people.

Smiling Mind

Smiling Mind develops evidence-based meditation and mindfulness exercises for both kids and adults, with the goal of alleviating stress and improving focus at work and in the classroom. Their meditation programs are suitable for schools and education. Smiling Mind acts to help reduce emotional reactivity and behavioural issues in school-aged kids.
